yield: 1
prepTime: 5min (2 hours rest time)

ingredients:
- 250g wheat flour (per bread)
- 80% warm water
- 6% olive oil
- 3% yeast
- 2.5% salt
- 0.5tsp sugar
- more olive oil or beaten egg for coating
- nigella seeds for coating
- sesame seeds for coating

directions:
- Rehydrate yeast by mixing it with some of the water and the sugar.
- Wait for yeast to foam.
- Separately mix all dry and all liquid ingredients (except for the coating ones).
- Mix everything until a smooth (quite wet) mass is formed.
- Cover the mixing bowl with cling film or a wet towel and wait an hour (until mass has doubled in size). Occasionally stretch-and-fold with wet hands.
- With more flour bring dough roughly into the desired shape (it will grow in diameter a bit) and put it on the baking tray.
- Again cover and wait an hour for final proofing.
- Coat with olive oil or beaten egg using a brush and sprinkle seeds on the top as desired.
- Bake at 8 minutes at 250°C (pre-heated), then turn heat down to 200°C and bake for another 5 minutes until golden brown.
- Wrap the bread in a towel or aluminium foil and let cool. Keep wrapped till serving time.
---

Base staple of turkish cuisine - you need this ready from breakfast till dinner time.
Despite the few ingredients (no yogurt, no baking agents) this turns out better than most recipes I tried thanks to the high-liquid
no-kneed approach.
