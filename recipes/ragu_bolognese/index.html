Ragu Bolognese

Ragù Bolognese, aka Sauce Bolognese known for it’s use with Spaghetti or in Lasagne.

The missing ingredient in 90% of all lacklustre sauce bolognese you ever tasted is patience. Those that still feel like something’s missing after that realization usually don’t know about salsiccia. If you are unable to get some you could try substituting it for more ground beef and the spices that usually go into salsiccia (mostly fennel), but I have never tried that.

  • Yields: 4
  • Preparation time: 15min
  • Cooking time: 3-4 hours
  • Sources: Video by J. Kenji López-Alt

Ingredients

  • 250g salsiccia
  • 250g ground beef
  • 1 medium sized, white onion
  • 1 carrot
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • 250ml red wine
  • black pepper
  • 2 cans of diced tomatoes
  • 250ml milk (or a little less cream instead)

Steps

  1. In a large pan roast the meat well.
  2. Add carrots, the onion and garlic.
  3. Before the garlic turns brown, add pepper and wine. Let simmer for a minute.
  4. Add tomatoes and milk and reduce until most of the liquid is gone and there are small isles of fat on the surface. This will probably take a few hours.
