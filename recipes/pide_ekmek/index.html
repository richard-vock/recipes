Pide Ekmek

Base staple of turkish cuisine - you need this ready from breakfast till dinner time. Despite the few ingredients (no yogurt, no baking agents) this turns out better than most recipes I tried thanks to the high-liquid no-kneed approach.

  • Yields: 1
  • Preparation time: 5min (2 hours rest time)

    Ingredients

    • 250g wheat flour (per bread)
    • 80% warm water
    • 6% olive oil
    • 3% yeast
    • 2.5% salt
    • 0.5tsp sugar
    • more olive oil or beaten egg for coating
    • nigella seeds for coating
    • sesame seeds for coating

    Steps

    1. Rehydrate yeast by mixing it with some of the water and the sugar.
    2. Wait for yeast to foam.
    3. Separately mix all dry and all liquid ingredients (except for the coating ones).
    4. Mix everything until a smooth (quite wet) mass is formed.
    5. Cover the mixing bowl with cling film or a wet towel and wait an hour (until mass has doubled in size). Occasionally stretch-and-fold with wet hands.
    6. With more flour bring dough roughly into the desired shape (it will grow in diameter a bit) and put it on the baking tray.
    7. Again cover and wait an hour for final proofing.
    8. Coat with olive oil or beaten egg using a brush and sprinkle seeds on the top as desired.
    9. Bake at 8 minutes at 250°C (pre-heated), then turn heat down to 200°C and bake for another 5 minutes until golden brown.
    10. Wrap the bread in a towel or aluminium foil and let cool. Keep wrapped till serving time.
