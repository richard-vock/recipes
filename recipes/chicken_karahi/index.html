Chicken Karahi

A pakistani street dish that is quick to make and brings some intense flavours. Of course other meats or vegetables can be used, I personally insist on serving it with soft wheat-based bread though.

  • Yields: 2
  • Preparation time: 30min

Ingredients

  • 500g boneless, skinless chickn thighs
  • Ghee
  • 1 can of tomatoes (whole or sliced)
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 2 tbsp minced ginger
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin
  • 1 tbsp garam masala
  • 1 tbsp cayenne
  • 0.5 tbsp turmeric
  • 0.5 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 0.5 tsp MSG
  • black pepper
  • a few chopped green chiles
  • a few strands of culantro or coriander

Steps

  1. In a mortar grind garlic and 1 tbsp of ginger – put aside.
  2. Mix dry spices.
  3. Cut the chicken thigh into large cubes.
  4. In a very hot pan or wok heat the ghee, salt and sauté the chicken until it starts to brown.
  5. A ginger and garlic paste, quickly stir and before it browns add the tomatoes.
  6. If using whole tomatoes crunch them and keep heating while often stirring until they start to thicken.
  7. Add spices and chiles and keep heating until the mix is thick and pasty. You can optionally add ghee or butter if desired.
  8. Before serving (without heating) mix in the other tbsp of ginger and chopped culantro (you can include the stems).
  9. Serve with Naan or Roti bread.
