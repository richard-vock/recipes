Dal (Template)

While originating in a pretty hot country Dals happen to be my favorite examples of a warming winter dish. When making 8 different Dal dishes (mostly following NYT cooking and Serious Eats) within two weeks I thought it makes more sense to write a recipe for the concept of this dish type as it follows a pretty consistent pattern.

There’s also 3 rules I found for myself:

  1. Don’t be afraid of the ghee. Add alot in the beginning. Then add more later. And maybe more after that. If you need your meal to have less fat make less and delegate its job to side dishes.
  2. Don’t be afraid for your Dal to look ugly. It is supposed to be a mushy, brown mess - if you absolutely need it to be impress, throw beautyful things onto it.
  3. Don’t be afraid to experiment with the spices. Never seen a recipe with allspice, or thai basil, but they resulted in impressive variants.

The recipe is per cup (150g) of lentils. In contrast to other recipes I actually just use a cup for measuring - this is about rough ratios, not precision. One cup yields two small servings. I usually use two cups and have one or two leftover portions for the freezer.

Serve with Naan bread and potentially side dishes.

Dals also react pretty forgiving to adding water or cooking longer to get rid of it. Don’t like the consistency? Fix it. I prefer it being on the threshold between a paste and a puree.

  • Yields: 4
  • Preparation time: 5min
  • Cooking time: 20min
  • Sources: Serious Eats, NYT Cooking

Ingredients

  • 3-4 tbsp of ghee
  • one medium sized yellow onion (or red)
  • piece of ginger (optional but highly recommended)
  • few cloves of garlic (optional)
  • spices – try cayenne, turmeric, coriander (ground or seeds), cumin (ground or seeds), black mustard seeds, asafoetida, galangal, chiles, tomato or paprika paste, pre-made spice mixes (like garam or tandoori masala) or anything else
  • 1 cup / 150gr of yellow or red lentils (try replacing a fraction with potatoes)
  • 3 cups of water
  • acids (e.g. lime juice, tamarind, vinegars)

Steps

  1. Add ghee to medium heat large pot, add onions and cook until brown. Stir often.
  2. Add any ginger, garlic and spices and heat until the smell makes your neighbors jealous (just don’t burn them, please).
  3. If you use pastes (e.g. tomato or paprika) or you pureed your ginger/garlic) at them now and heat until the water is evaporated.
  4. Add lentils and water and cook until consistency is as desired (anything from 20 to 60 minutes). Stir occasionally.
  5. Sensitive spices like cinnamon, nutmeg or allspice are best added at the end. Cook for a few more minutes with stirring to have them mingle with rest of the gang.
  6. Add acidic components, salt and sugar to taste.
