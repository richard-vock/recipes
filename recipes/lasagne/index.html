Lasagna

Who doesn’t enjoy a good lasagna. And it even gets better once it gets cold.

Don’t try to counterbalance a bad ragu by using lots of cheese - the ragu makes the taste, the bechamel the creaminess and the cheese isn’t even necessary once you get the former two right.

The missing ingredient in 90% of all lacklustre sauce bolognese you ever tasted is patience. Those that still feel like something’s missing after that realization usually don’t know about salsiccia. If you are unable to get some you could try substituting it for more ground beef and the spices that usually go into salsiccia (mostly fennel), but I have never tried that.

  • Preparation time: 15min
  • Cooking time: 45min
  • Sources: Video by J. Kenji López-Alt

Ingredients

  • lasagne plates
  • as desired ragù bolognese (prepare before)
  • as desired bechamel (prepare in parallel)
  • (optionally) ricotta or other cheese

Steps

  1. let plates soak in luke-warm water for a while – meanwhile you can prepare the bechamel sauce if necessary.
  2. once the plates are soft, stack layers of ragù, bechamel and plates (start with ragù and then alternate all 3 and finish with a sauce).
  3. if desired, add a final layer of cheese.
  4. bake for 45 minutes at 180°C.
